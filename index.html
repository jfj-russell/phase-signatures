Quantum Tunnelling Without a Barrier

Welcome! This page contains animated figures from my project "Quantum Tunnelling Without a Barrier: Phase-Sensitive Signatures in High-Harmonic Generation (HHG)". The work explores how tunnelling dynamics can arise even without a classical potential barrier by looking at the phase behaviour of high harmonics produced in two-colour laser fields.

While the phase coefficients in the monochromatic case are well understood, the two-colour variations presented here are new results, showing distinctive phase-structure features and trajectory interactions not seen before.

If you know of any tools or frameworks for displaying interactive Julia plots, do let me know!

Two-colour switchover configurations

A switchover from ω to 2ω. Tunnelling without a barrier happens at the second ionisation event (green) when the components have equal amplitude. The total electric field shown here is given by

E ( t ) = E 1 cos ( ω t ) + E 2 cos ( 2 ω t + ϕ )

Harmonic variation of α vs laser intensity

Quantum-path phase coefficients over a scan of total laser intensity for a strongly bichromatic field. The varied quantity in the gif is the emitted harmonic. The branchings and cutoffs here correspond to partial coalescences between trajectories or to the termination of a trajectory when the harmonic cutoff is reached.

α surfaces for tunnelling without a barrier

Quantum-path phase coefficient surfaces over an independent field component grid. Again the animation shows the harmonic variation. The surface structure indicates the order of dependence of the phase sensitivity. I think it looks like dancing stingrays :)
